Rewatch-Worthy: My Top 5 Picks

These aren't Oscar winners (well, maybe one), and they're not here to impress anyone. They're just my all-time favorite movies and series — the ones I throw on when I need a comfort watch, a good laugh, or something familiar to vibe with. Think of this list as my personal greatest hits: no ranking, no pressure, just pure watch-again-and-again energy.

#1

Solo Levelling

OVERVIEW:

Sung Jin-woo begins as the weakest Hunter in a world of “Gates” and magical threats. After gaining a mysterious ability to level up independently, he transforms into one of the most powerful fighters alive, battling monsters and uncovering hidden conspiracies.

Release: January 6, 2024

Genre: Action, Fantasy, Adventure, Anime

Cast: In the Japanese version, the main voice actors include Taito Ban, Reina Ueda, Genta Nakamura, Daisuke Hirakawa, Hiroki Tōchi, Banjō Ginga, and Makoto Furukawa. In the English dub, the cast features Aleks Le, Michelle Rojas, Justin Briner, Ian Sinclair, Christopher R. Sabat, Rebecca Wang, and SungWon Cho.

Duration: ~24 minutes per episode

Country: Japan & South Korea

Production: A-1 Pictures; directed by Shunsuke Nakashige, written by Noboru Kimura, character design by Tomoko Sudo, music by Hiroyuki Sawano

IMDB: 8.6/10 (based on ~85K votes)

#2

Sandman

OVERVIEW:

An adaptation of Neil Gaiman's seminal comics: Dream (Morpheus) embarks on a quest to restore his realm, face divine and monstrous enemies, and mend connections severed by past mistakes

Release: Season 1 premiered August 5, 2022; final Season 2 released in two volumes in July 2025 (Vol 1: July 3; Vol 2: July 24; plus a special bonus episode July 31)

Genre: Fantasy Drama, Supernatural

Cast: Tom Sturridge (Dream), Boyd Holbrook (The Corinthian), Vivienne Acheampong (Lucienne), Patton Oswalt (Matthew the Raven), plus returning characters like Kirby Howell-Baptiste, Mason Alexander Park, etc.

Duration: ~37-65 minutes per episode

Country: United Kingdom / United States

Production: PurePop Inc., The Blank Corporation, Phantom Four, DC Entertainment, Warner Bros. Television

IMDB: Not directly listed, but review sites cite positive reception; game-based consensus at ~74% Rotten Tomatoes for Season 2

#3

Demon Slayer (Kimetsu no Yaiba)

OVERVIEW:

After demons attack his family, Tanjiro's sister Nezuko becomes one. To avenge his family and cure Nezuko, Tanjiro becomes a Demon Slayer and embarks on a fierce journey across Japan

Release: Season 1 premiered April 6, 2019; multiple subsequent seasons through June 2024

Genre: Action, Fantasy, Adventure, Anime

Cast: Natsuki Hanae, Akari Kitō, Zach Aguilar, Abby Trott, Bryce Papenbrook, Zeno Robinson

Duration: ~24 minutes per episode

Country: Japan

Production: Ufotable, directed by Haruo Sotozaki, produced by Hikaru Kondo

IMDB: 8.6/10 (same ballpark as Solo Leveling; ranked top anime)

#4

Bridgerton

OVERVIEW:

Set in Regency-era London, follows the Bridgerton family's romantic and social endeavors, blending historical romance with modern sensibilities and diverse casting, each season focusing on a different sibling's love story

Release: Season 1 debuted December 25, 2020; subsequent seasons released into mid-2024; Season 3 Part 1 (May 16, 2024) and Part 2 (June 13, 2024)

Genre: Historical Romance, Drama

Cast: Phoebe Dynevor (Daphne), Regé-Jean Page (Simon, Season 1), plus ensemble across seasons (Anthony, Kate, etc.)

Duration: ~55-60 minutes per episode (typical for Netflix drama)

Country: United Kingdom / United States (Netflix)

Production: Shondaland (Chris Van Dusen), produced by Netflix; executive producers include Shonda Rhimes

IMDB: 7.4/10 (based on ~176K votes)

#5

Modern Family

OVERVIEW:

A mockumentary sitcom following the connected yet unique lives of a multi-generational, blended family living in suburban Los Angeles, exploring everyday joys and challenges with heart and humor

Release: First aired September 23, 2009; last aired April 8, 2020

Genre: Sitcom, Mockumentary

Cast: Ed O'Neill, Sofía Vergara, Julie Bowen, Ty Burrell, Jesse Tyler Ferguson, Eric Stonestreet, Sarah Hyland, Ariel Winter, Nolan Gould, Rico Rodriguez, Aubrey Anderson-Emmons, Jeremy Maguire, Reid Ewing

Duration: ~24 minutes per episode

Country: United States

Production: Lloyd-Levitan, Picador Productions, Steven Levitan Productions, 20th Century Fox Television

IMDB: Not directly in source, but widely rated around 8.4-8.5/10; hugely awarded show including 22 Emmys and a Golden Globe
